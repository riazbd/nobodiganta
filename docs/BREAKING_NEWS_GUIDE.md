# Breaking News — How It Works

A simple guide to the breaking news feature, for editors and the newsroom team.
No technical knowledge needed.

---

## What it does

Breaking news lets you put an important headline in front of every reader the
moment it happens — shown as a highlighted bar across the site — and take it down
automatically when it's no longer urgent. You control everything from one place
in the admin panel.

---

## What readers see (the public site)

- **A breaking news bar** appears across the site (along the bottom) with your
  headline scrolling in it.
- The bar's **look changes with how urgent the news is**:
  - **Just In** – a quiet, low-key style for minor updates.
  - **Breaking** – the classic red bar.
  - **Urgent** – a flashing red bar that demands attention.
  - **Live** – a green bar with a pulsing dot, for ongoing/live coverage.
- Clicking the headline takes the reader to the full story (or to wherever you
  pointed it).
- **It updates on its own.** If you post or change breaking news, readers see it
  within a few seconds — they don't need to refresh the page.
- **Readers can close the bar.** If someone dismisses it, it stays hidden for
  them until you post something new.
- There's also a **"Breaking" page** that lists recent breaking items, in case a
  reader wants to catch up.
- It works in **both the Bangla and English editions**, and you choose which
  edition(s) each item appears in.

---

## How you manage it (the admin panel)

Everything lives in **Breaking News** in the admin menu (you'll also find a
"Breaking News" shortcut on the dashboard).

### Posting breaking news — two ways

1. **From an existing article** — pick a published story and promote it to
   breaking. The headline and link are filled in for you.
2. **Standalone (quick alert)** — type a short headline yourself, without writing
   a full article. Perfect for fast-moving situations ("Cabinet meeting called for
   5 PM"). You can optionally point it to a link.

### What you can set for each item

- **Urgency level** – Just In, Breaking, Urgent, or Live (this controls how the
  public bar looks).
- **Edition** – Bangla, English, or both.
- **Pin** – keep an important item at the front of the line.
- **Order/Priority** – arrange which items show first when there are several.
- **Start time** – schedule an item to appear later automatically.
- **Expiry time** – set when it should automatically disappear (see below).

### It cleans up after itself

This is the key benefit: breaking news **doesn't go stale**. If you give an item
an expiry time, it removes itself from the site automatically when that time
passes — no one has to remember to take it down. You can also **expire it
instantly** with one click, or **bring it back** if needed.

### Organised into three tabs

- **Active** – what's live on the site right now.
- **Scheduled** – set to go live later.
- **Expired** – past items (kept for reference; can be reactivated).

### A quick shortcut

On the **All News** list, each article has a small **Breaking** switch. Flipping
it on instantly makes that story a breaking item (with the standard red style);
flipping it off removes it. The full Breaking News page is where you go for more
control (urgency level, expiry, scheduling, standalone alerts).

## In short

- Post breaking news in seconds — from a story or as a quick alert.
- Choose how urgent it looks, which edition it shows in, and when it expires.
- Readers see it live, styled by urgency, and it clears itself automatically.
- One place to manage it all, with a quick on/off switch on every article.
