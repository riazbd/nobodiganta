# Breaking News — User Guide

A complete, plain-language guide to the Breaking News feature: what your readers
see, and how your team posts and manages it. No technical knowledge needed.

---

## 1. Overview

Breaking News lets your newsroom push an important headline in front of **every
reader instantly** — shown as a highlighted bar across the website — and have it
**clear itself automatically** when it's no longer urgent.

You control everything from one screen in the admin panel. You can post breaking
news from an existing article, or type a quick standalone alert in seconds.

There are **two sides** to it:

- **The public site** — where readers see the breaking bar.
- **The admin panel** — where your team creates and manages it.

---

## 2. What your readers see (the public site)

### The breaking bar
A strip pinned to the **bottom of every page** on the website. Your headline
scrolls across it. It appears automatically when there is active breaking news,
and disappears on its own when there is none.

- Clicking the headline opens the full story (or any link you set).
- It works on phones, tablets and computers.

### Four urgency styles
The bar restyles itself based on the urgency level you choose for each item:

| Level | How the bar looks | When to use it |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Just In** | Quiet grey label | Minor updates, low-key items |
| **Breaking** | Classic red bar | Standard breaking news |
| **Urgent** | Flashing red bar | Major, attention-demanding news |
| **Live** | Green bar with a pulsing dot | Ongoing / live coverage |

If several items are active at once, the bar takes on the **most urgent** one's look.

### Updates by itself (live)
When you post or change breaking news, readers see the update within a few
seconds — **they do not need to refresh the page.**

### Readers can close it
There is a small **✕ button at the right end** of the bar. A reader can tap it to
hide the bar; it returns automatically when you post something new.

### The Breaking page
There is a dedicated page listing recent breaking news. Readers reach it from the
**"Breaking News" link under the "More ▾" menu** in the main navigation (and from
the **mobile menu**), or directly at **`/breaking`** (**`/en/breaking`** for the
English edition). They can use it to catch up on recent breaking items.

### Bangla & English editions
The Bangla bar appears on the main site; the English bar appears on the English
site (`/en`). For each item you choose **Bangla, English, or Both**, which decides
where it shows.

## 3. How your team posts breaking news (the admin panel)

Open **Breaking News** from the left-hand admin menu (there's also a "Breaking
News" shortcut on the dashboard).

### Two ways to post

**A) From an existing article**
1. Click **New**.
2. Choose **From Article** and search for the published story.
3. (Optional) adjust the headline, urgency, edition, etc.
4. Save. The headline and link are filled in for you.

**B) Standalone quick alert** (no full article needed)
1. Click **New**.
2. Choose **Standalone**.
3. Type a short headline (Bangla and/or English). Optionally add a link.
4. Choose the urgency and edition, then Save.

> Use standalone alerts for fast-moving situations — e.g. *"Cabinet meeting
> called for 5 PM"* — when there isn't time to write a full article yet.

### Options you can set for each item
- **Urgency level** — Just In / Breaking / Urgent / Live (controls the bar's look).
- **Edition** — Bangla, English, or Both.
- **Pin** — keep an important item at the front of the line.
- **Priority/Order** — arrange which items show first when there are several.
- **Start time** — schedule an item to appear automatically later.
- **Expiry time** — set when it should automatically disappear.

### It cleans up after itself
This is the key benefit — **breaking news never goes stale.**
- Set an **expiry time** and the item removes itself from the site when that time
  passes. No one has to remember to take it down.
- You can also **Expire it instantly** with one click, or **Reactivate** it later.

### The three tabs
- **Active** — what's live on the site right now.
- **Scheduled** — set to appear later.
- **Expired** — past items (kept for reference; can be brought back).

### Quick on/off switch on every article
On the **All News** list, each article has a small **Breaking** switch. Turning it
on instantly makes that story breaking (standard red style); turning it off
removes it. For full control — urgency level, expiry, scheduling, standalone
alerts — use the Breaking News screen.

---

## 4. Recommended workflow

1. **Big news just happened?** Post a **Standalone** alert immediately (a few
   seconds) so readers know right away.
2. **Writing the full story?** Once published, either promote it from the article,
   or flip the **Breaking** switch on the article — then expire the standalone
   alert.
3. **Always set an expiry** (e.g. a few hours) so the bar stays fresh and relevant.
4. **Reserve "Urgent" and "Live"** for genuinely major or ongoing events, so they
   keep their impact.

---

## 5. Frequently asked questions

**Do readers have to refresh to see new breaking news?**
No. The bar updates on its own within seconds.

**What happens if I forget to remove an item?**
If you set an expiry time, it disappears automatically. You can also expire it
instantly anytime.

**Can I show different breaking news on the Bangla and English sites?**
Yes — set each item's edition to Bangla, English, or Both.

**A reader closed the bar — is it gone forever?**
No. It returns for them as soon as you post new breaking news.

**Will readers get a notification on their phone?**
Phone/browser push notifications are **built but switched off for now**, and will
be enabled in a future update. No action is needed from you until then.

---

## 6. In short

- Post breaking news in seconds — from a story or as a quick alert.
- Choose how urgent it looks, which edition it appears in, and when it expires.
- Readers see it live at the bottom of every page; it clears itself automatically.
- Manage everything from **Admin → Breaking News**, with a quick switch on every
  article.
